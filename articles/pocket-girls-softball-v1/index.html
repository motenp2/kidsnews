Pocket Girls Softball V1: My First Season With the Blue Crushers (and Alani With the Glove Bugs)

By Sarai Moten, age 8 May 4, 2026 5 MIN READ

Hi, my name is Sarai, and this season I played softball for the Blue Crushers, number 4. My little sister Alani is 5, and she played for the Glove Bugs. This was our very first season with Pocket Girls Softball in the Pocket part of Sacramento, and we already feel like we belong on the field.

Pocket Girls Softball is a league where girls in our neighborhood get to play, learn, and have fun. The website shows the whole structure with divisions like T-Ball, 8U, 10U, 12U, 14U, and 16U, and games and practices happen in the Pocket. Alani plays in the little division with the Glove Bugs (best name ever, tiny but mighty), and I play 8U with the Blue Crushers. Our coach is Coach Mariko, and our assistant coaches are Nick, Andy, and Aron. My teammates are Aricela #25, Briella #10, Calliope #88, Camila #30, Dylan #8, Emery #24, Emma #12, Finley #99, Lucia #2, Meilani #7, Mya #90, and Olivia #22. Blue team, blue socks, blue everything.

We Love Batting

Both of us LOVE batting. When I step into the box, helmet on, bat up, I am not scared of the pitch. I am waiting for it. Alani is the same way. She is 5 years old and she walks up to the plate like she owns it, pink cleats and all. We both love that crack sound and the run to first base.

We Are Great in the Field

We are also great in the field. I like scooping grounders and making the throw to first. Alani chases every ball that comes near her like it is her job. The coaches say we have good hands, and we believe them. Glove down, eyes on the ball, ready position. That is how we do it.

And We Are FAST

Like, really fast. When I get on base I look for the next one. When Alani runs, the whole crowd starts cheering because her little legs go zoom. Speed is our superpower. Coach says good base runners change the whole game, and we plan to change a lot of games.

“The dugout cheers, the sunflower seeds, the bubble gum — that is what makes Pocket Girls Softball special.”
— Sarai, Blue Crushers #4

The Whole Pocket Softball World

The best part is not just the games. It is the WHOLE softball world in the Pocket. The dugout cheers. The sunflower seeds. The bubble gum. The team banners with our names and numbers on them. The families in camping chairs yelling “GO! GO! GO!” The little sisters watching the big sisters, and the big sisters teaching the little ones. That is what makes Pocket Girls Softball special. It is not just a league, it is a community.

Next Year We Are Veterans

This was our first season, but do not get it twisted. We hit, we fielded, we ran, and we showed up every single game ready to play. Next year, Alani and I are coming back as veterans. We will know the fields, the cheers, the chants, and the snack bar by heart. And we will still be fast.

Watch out, Pocket. The Moten sisters are just getting started.
