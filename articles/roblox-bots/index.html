The Secret Language of Roblox Bots

By Alani Moten April 18, 2026 4 MIN READ

Hi! I'm Alani and I'm five years old. My sister Sarai lets me watch her play Roblox sometimes, and I noticed something strange — there are players in the game that don't act like real people. They move in funny patterns, repeat the same words, and never stop. My sister told me those are called bots, and they're made by AI. I had so many questions!

My father explained that bots are computer programs that pretend to be players. In Roblox, some bots are used to test games, but others are used to cause trouble — like spamming the chat or trying to trick kids into clicking bad links. The people who make Roblox are always working to find and remove the bad bots, but it's like a game inside the game.

“AI is shaping the future of digital play — and learning to spot what’s real is the most important skill a kid gamer can have.”
— Alani Moten, age 5

What I find really interesting is that AI isn't just making bad bots — it's also making the game smarter. AI helps create characters that react to what you do, makes the world feel more alive, and can even help designers build new maps and levels faster. My sister says that someday games might be so smart they can tell your own unique story every time you play.

I think it's important for kids who love games to understand what AI is doing inside them. When you know how bots work, you can spot them and protect yourself. And when you understand how AI builds games, maybe you'll want to learn to build them too. I know I do — when I'm older, I want to make a Roblox world where you can have a pet dragon!
